For Logistics and Fleet Management, **geopolitical events and labor strikes are among the biggest external risk factors** because they directly affect routes, fuel costs, delivery times, fleet utilization, and profitability.

## Major Geopolitical Risks

### 1. Maritime Chokepoint Disruptions

Critical routes:

* Strait of Hormuz
* Red Sea
* Suez Canal
* Panama Canal

Recent conflicts in the Middle East have disrupted shipping routes, increased fuel costs, and forced carriers to reroute vessels around Africa, increasing transit times and costs. Current tensions involving Iran and threats to Red Sea shipping continue to create uncertainty for global logistics networks. ([Reuters][1])

### Business Impact

* Increased freight rates
* Longer transit times
* Higher fuel costs
* Inventory shortages
* Reduced asset utilization

# Enterprise Example

Consider a shipment:

```text
Mumbai
   ↓
Hyderabad
   ↓
Bangalore
```

Suddenly:

```text
Heavy rainfall near Hyderabad
Road closed
```

Agentic workflow:

```text
Monitor Agent detects closure
          ↓
Impact Agent calculates delay
          ↓
Replanning Agent creates
3 alternative routes
          ↓
Optimization Agent scores options
          ↓
Execution Agent updates route
          ↓
Customer Notification Agent
sends revised ETA
```

This is the core difference between traditional logistics software and Agentic AI: the system doesn't just raise an alert—it autonomously evaluates alternatives, replans, and executes the optimal course of action while keeping humans in the loop where required.
